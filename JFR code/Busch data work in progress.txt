Plan to take data from “Cost-effectiveness of natural forest regeneration and plantations for climate mitigation,” Busch et al, into SMDAMAGE

1. Create a database of their data.
	Table Busch2024_undiscounted_dta_output
    • Country, pixel id, genus, values A and k for best harvest type, 
    • best harvest type: natural regeneration, native plantation, or exotic plantation.
    • Y = harvest year or planning horizon, mapping to contract length.
    • areaid  area in hectares,
		◦ crop_va: opportunity cost/hectare/year. I will discount crop_va over the length of the contract to the start of the contract, 
			as NPV(contract length Y, crop_va) = crop_va*sum{t=0 to Y}(1 – discount_rate)^t. 
			SMDAMAGE will then discount it again from the planting year back to the 2026.
			Because I run different discount rates in SMDAMAGE, I store this discounted value for multiple discount rates.
		◦ nr_cost: natural regeneration planting cost per hectare in 2020 USD,
		◦ np_cost: native plantation planting cost per hectare in 2020 USD,
		◦ ep_cost: exotic plantation planting cost per hectare in 2020 USD,
		◦ p: revenue from wood products (net of harvest and delivery costs) as a scalar of carbon at time of harvest. 
    Then bid cost per hectare: NPV(contract length Y, crop_va) + nr_cost + ep_cost + np_cost – p. 
	Two of these will be zero, because we commit to either natural regeneration or exotic plantation.

		Must accrue only once for each contract? I guess the auction manager could buy a contract where the supplier repeatedly harvested the trees.
	Table Busch2024_undiscounted_tC_per_h_year
    • Carbon removal per hectare in each year 1, …, Y.

2. Given the above data, I want to categorize carbon removal per hectare into maybe 50 bins. Each bin has total Areap in millions of hectares.
To do this binning, I need the full solution database. Then I will query it to categorize the carbon removal schedules.

I used JFR code/2_k_means_carbon_removal.py to compress pixel-level carbon-removal schedules into forestry bidders for SMDAMAGE. 
The k-means algorithm clustered pixels within each selected_rotation_year on year-by-year tC_per_ha_per_year profiles. 
I then used JFR code/1_import_k_means_csv_to_sqlite.py to load those cluster centers and assignments into Output/Busch2024_to_SMDAMAGE.sqlite.
The database stores the cluster centers in table carbon_removal_schedules and each pixel receives a cluster_index. 
Next, I used JFR code/4_move_Busch_data_to_SMDAMAGE.py to export bidder metadata (forestry_bidder_metadata.csv) 
and bidder-specific bid-step curves (forestry_bid_steps_long.csv) from per-pixel costs and areas. 
In that export step, each (rotation_year, cluster_index) pair is one bidder, summed area becomes available_area_mhectares, 
and bid steps are built by sorting per-pixel bid_cost_per_ha and accumulating area. 
This keeps SMDAMAGE tractable while preserving heterogeneity in carbon timing, land availability, and costs.
